Differential piston aberrations, commonly known as petaling, represent a critical limitation for the optical performance of the Extremely Large Telescopes (ELT). If left uncorrected, petaling severely degrades the core of the point spread function (PSF), restricting high-contrast imaging capabilities and reducing the spatial resolution of a segmented primary mirror to that of a single sub-aperture petal. In this paper, we present an evolution of our deep-learning framework dedicated to estimating differential piston modes. By replacing our previous sub-PSF approach with a method exploiting defocused dual-wavelength focal-plane images, we successfully enhance both the capture range performance and the overall versatility of the wavefront sensor. First, the network can concurrently estimate all five differential piston modes of a 6-petal pupil from a single image frame, introducing native robustness against pupil rotation. Under realistic ELT turbulence residuals (25% Strehl ratio baseline with 13-frame averaging), the network achieves a wavefront error of approximately 25 nm RMS, demonstrating very close agreement with historical frameworks (23 nm RMSE). Second, by incorporating a dual-wavelength input configuration (λ1 = 1.5 µm and λ2 = 1.8 µm), the network expands its operational bounds well beyond the classical [−π,π] phase ambiguity. Robust linear estimation is maintained up to a range of [−5π,5π]. Beyond the synthetic wavelength limit (Λ = 9.0,µm), the system continues to exhibit a coarse linear behavior, with no clear evidence of classical phase wrapping. Finally, we outline our roadmap for experimental validation on the High-Contrast Module (HCM) testbed at IPAG using the Zernike wavefront sensor as a reference baseline, paving the way for eventual on-sky deployment on the EKARUS adaptive optics R&D platform at the Asiago Observatory.
